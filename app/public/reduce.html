Reducing Home Energy Consumption to Maximize Solar Power Profits

  • Reza Jafery
  • 20 Oct 2018
  • 1,214
Share Tweet Pin Email

If you’ve gone through all the trouble of setting up an energy efficient solar power system for your home, you might as well make sure you’re making the most additional income you can from it. When you ask someone how to generate more money from a solar panel setup, the most common answer is simply, “Add more panels” or “Upgrade your solar panels and batteries”. When in reality, the first step should be to cut down energy consumption in your home to as low as possible.

Home appliances account for 20% of the energy consumed by the average person.

I’m personally not of the belief that implementing solar energy means that you should also cut down on the activities you enjoy that consume energy. Instead, I think we should focus on making sure the appliances around your home and the other elements that consume energy within it are as energy efficient as possible.

Solar Appliances

The only thing more effective than energy efficient appliances are solar powered appliances that don’t draw additional power from your solar panels. If they can operate completely independent of your power grid (Off the grid of your “off the grid” system!) then they won’t create any unnecessary energy burdens that reduce the revenue you’re generating through your solar energy system.

You can find solar powered:

  • Refrigerators
  • Freezers
  • Ovens
  • Chargers


Solar Chargers are really useful because you can purchase a few of these and use them to power your traditional home appliances. A solar charger is a device that you set outside to collect energy, and can then bring into your home and use as a power source for other devices. This requires minimal effort to create another power source for your appliances, so that you can decrease the energy load on your solar power grid.

Focusing on Energy Efficiency in Your Home

If you don’t want to get a solar powered appliance, then check out some of the more energy efficient appliances available. There are products out there that focus on consuming the least energy possible, without sacrificing quality and utility of the product itself. Check out ENERGY STAR for some of the most efficient kitchen appliances out there. Start off with with a refrigerator, dishwasher, dryers, and washing machine— these are typically the largest consumers of energy in a kitchen.

Once you’ve knocked out the kitchen, take a look at some of the energy efficient heating and cooling systems available. Virtually anything in your home has an energy efficient version- unless you’re already using the most efficient. If so, kudos, this article isn’t for you!

Understanding that every energy consuming device in your home can probably be more efficient means that you have to look at whatever appliances consume the most electricity in your home and prioritize them in order of what your family uses the most. Take a look at a break down of your power consumption or just think about what your family does the most. If you have a few kids who are always watching TV and playing video games, a TV and entertainment system might be the first thing to tackle. If your family plays a lot of sports and goes through clean clothes like crazy, then your washer and dryer might be the first appliances to optimize for energy efficiency.
