Before You Set Up Your Renewable Energy System: Consumption Analysis and Zoning Requirements

  • Reza Jafery
  • 4 Nov 2018
  • 559
Share Tweet Pin Email

There are a series of questions you need to ask yourself before pulling the trigger on a renewable energy system for your home. There are an abundance of reasons to make the switch from grid power to off-the-grid power: perhaps you want to save money by setting up a small solar grid on your roof, or maybe you want to set up a small wind electric system in your backyard to take advantage of the wide open planes of your rural area. Regardless of your reasoning, or even what kind of energy set up youd like to use dont spend a dollar until youve gone through the following items and planned ahead for your setup.

Determining Your Current Electricity Usage

The first step is to figure out how much electricity your using (this is called your electrical load) and figure out if there are any places where you can reduce your current load through using more efficient appliances or other tactics. Finding this information will help you decide: the size of the renewable energy system youre going to set up, how much you can spend on it, how your energy requirements change over the course of time and when you need the most energy, and finally where you currently spend the most electricity; so you can reduce your total load.

You can get the majority of this information from your current power company login into your online bill payment portal and you should be able to find a detailed breakdown of your current electrical load. If youd like more information than is provided online, call your electric provider and theyll give you a in depth report on where and when youre consuming the most electricity.

When trying to determine which devices within your home are using the most electricity, follow this simple formula to find out how much power each appliance is consuming on a regular basis.

(Wattage) X (Hours an appliance is used per day) = daily electrical load of an appliance

Now that youve found out the size of your electrical load, try to identify certain areas where you can cut down your energy consumption- the less power you consume the more youll end up saving and potentially making in the future.

Side note: the best time to plan your renewable energy set up is when youre building your home. If youre constructing your own home and want an off-the-grid renewable energy system then ask the team youre working with to build your home to ensure it will be able to handle the system youd like.

Make Sure Youre Not Breaking Any Rules

There are different rules and regulations for setting up renewable energy systems across different states, and sometimes even across different towns and communities. The regulations in place by your local community play a part in determining what kind of renewable energy system you can use. Some neighborhoods dont want wind turbines spinning around constantly and making noise, and some buildings have specific codes that prevent tenants from adding to the actual building structure- preventing the addition of solar panels. If your building will allow a renewable energy system installation, theyre definitely going to have building inspectors checking it out, so its best to plan ahead to meet all of these requirements and avoid any future headaches

The quickest way to figure all of this out is to reach out to a renewable energy company that services your state and ask them regardless of whether or not you end up purchasing a system from them, theyll be happy to answer your questions. Make sure there are no covenants in your town that restrict homeowners from using renewable energy systems. Sometimes neighborhoods dont want renewable energy systems because they arent always the most aesthetically pleasing structures.

Easements

An easement is a government enforced and legally binding agreement issued by a state government. The agreement is between homeowners/land owners and the owners of the land surrounding them. You want to get an easement if you live in an area where youre concerned new construction might come up in the future and block your renewable energy system. For example, if you spend thousands of dollars on a spiffy solar grid system for your roof and they build a skyscraper a couple blocks away- it might end up drastically reducing the amount of sunlight that hits your roof on a daily basis. Reach out to your state government (or just ask the local renewable energy company) to find out if you can get an easement to protect your setup.

Once youve finished analyzing your electrical load and determined the necessary hurdles youll have to jump over to install your setup, you need to determine if your system will be grid-connected or completely off-the-grid, and finally; decide what kind of renewable energy technology youll use.
