The Financial Benefits of Switching to Solar

  • Reza Jafery
  • 5 Nov 2018
  • 750
Share Tweet Pin Email

With the rise of electricity costs going up each passing year, more and more people are looking to switch from local utility companies, and onto alternative power sources such as solar energy. Folks that switch over, notice a huge financial burden lifted off their shoulders, giving them ease throughout the summer and winter months where air conditioning and heat is a must. Solar technology has advanced so well that costs of installing a solar energy system can actually save you money by way of government tax incentives and various purchase/finance/lease options. And those sunny states where sunshine is abundant, harvesting those natural resources can put a pretty penny in your pocket. So if you’re looking to switch over from your utility company’s grasp to an alternative power source, here are three important financial benefits to keep in mind.

Save Money, Make Money

The most logical reason to switching over is it can lower your monthly bills and often times can make you money in the process. Solar panels generate their own power, which means this is energy you aren’t having to purchase from a utility company, so the more power your solar energy creates, the less money you have to spend elsewhere, leaving a nice chunk in your savings. And what is even better, is that if your utility provider supports net metering, you can end up making money off your system. When your solar panel system creates more energy than needed, that power is sent back to the grid and redistributed to users, allowing you to make money of your excess energy.

Home Value Increases

We all want to know our house will increase in value when the time to move on comes along. Installing solar panels can do just that. Solar power is one home improvement that entices buyers into buying your home because it not only has the potential to reward them every month, but gives them a sound investment to a home. The value of your home can increase by at least the cost of the system itself, if not more, and although you wouldn’t get this benefit immediately, knowing your house will be favored by many and worth more when you decide to sell is just as beneficial.

Incentives

You can get money back on your purchase or a cash incentive to go out and buy a solar panel system. Many of these incentives gives you your money back through tax credits that can range from 30-50%, and many states give a $1000 or higher incentive for each solar panel system installed. With incentives and tax breaks for installing systems, most people only end up paying half of the cost for solar panel systems.

Not only do you get the perks of incentives and savings, but you become energy independent rather than giving your hard earned money to the utility company every month. It’s a win-win situation. You get out of the money hungry pockets of utility companies, and make money while doing it.
