Home Solar 101: Things to Know Before Setting Up Your Solar Grid

  • Reza Jafery
  • 10 Oct 2018
  • 2,999
Share Tweet Pin Email

One of the first roadblocks people face when considering making the switch to solar energy is a lack of understanding. Despite all of the developments in solar technology, there isn’t a lot of credible information out there to give would-be solar powered individuals the background they need to figure out if solar is even right for them in the first place.

Before thinking about setting up a solar energy system in your home, let’s talk about what solar electricity actually is, the different types of solar power systems, and which of these systems are right for you.

Understanding Solar Powered Electricity

An acronym you need to get familiar with if you want to become a green energy guru is PV. PV stands for Photovoltaic and PV modules are the devices that actually gather and collect solar power and convert it into electricity. These are what most people call solar panels. Solar panels (or PVs) are often set up in a grid or “array” that are placed on top of homes or in areas that experience a large amount of sunlight.

I won’t get into all the science behind it, but in essence, there are basically two kinds of PV cells/modules: crystalline silicon modules and thin-film modules. There have been a lot of developments around thin-film modules and they’re often heralded as the future of solar energy, however the majority of solar energy currently generated is done through crystalline silicon modules.

Regardless of what type of cell or module you decide to use, you’ll put them into a grid or array, and connect these grids to inverters, charge controllers, and batteries— but we’ll touch more on that later. First, let’s look at the different types of solar-electric systems.

Off The Grid Systems

If you live in a rural area this might be the solar-electric system you’re looking for. They can be set up essentially anywhere, but bring the most benefit to individuals who live far away from power-grids. In order to have a completely off the grid solar electric system you’re going to need a battery to store energy— because, well, the sun don’t shine 24 hours a day! In Off The Grid systems, you need a battery, a charge controller, inverters, and of course; protective gear to make sure you don’t hurt yourself setting it up!

If you truly want to become 100% self sufficient and off the grid, this is the type of solar-electric system you want. With an off the grid system you don’t ever have to worry about blackouts because you don’t rely on other organizations for your power.

If you want to set up an off the grid system, but also want to connect to a utility grid just in case, simply connect your system to the local utility grid— the downside of this is that if the power goes out, so does your system.

PV Direct Systems

The most basic form of solar-electric setup is a PV Direct System. This consists of the least parts and is usually the easiest to set up. PV Direct systems only generate energy when the sun in shining because they don’t use batteries to store excess energy. Although far more simple to set up, they aren’t the most robust system to use if you’re looking to generate your own energy, or even take it a step further and generate revenue from your solar setup.

Most people use an Off-The-Grid System without a battery, meaning that they still rely on the local power grid, but offset their costs by generating electricity as well.

Before you take the dive into building your own solar-electric system (or having a company set one up for you), decide if you want to be completely off the grid, or still be able to fall back on the power grid.
