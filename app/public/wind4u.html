Is a Wind Energy System Right For You?

  • Reza Jafery
  • 20 October 2018
  • 908
Share Tweet Pin Email

Most of the news we hear about renewable energy sources focuses around solar power. Although its often the most reasonable method for generating energy in warm climates, there are other options if you dont live in a sunny locale.

If you want to stop relying on government funded sources of power and truly become self-sufficient then wind power might be a viable option for your energy needs.

Although not as affordable or easy to implement as solar power, wind, much like the sun, is one of the most reliable forms of energy that exists. Whether rain, sleet, or snow the wind still blows. Whether or not wind flow is strong enough in your area however is something youll have to think about when determining if this is the right choice for you. Before you go any further, know that building a wind power system is going to require a lot of square footage and these will more than likely only be feasible in open rural areas.

There are three main elements to consider when deciding if you want to implement a wind power system.

Is There Enough Wind In Your Area?

The first thing aspiring wind powered individuals will need to figure out is if there is enough wind in their area to power the system. There are many variables that you have to take into account here, things such as terrain, time of year, general climate, and several other elements all contribute to the amount of wind resource available in your area.

To figure out if theres enough wind in your area, consult Wind Resource Maps, these are used to figure out how much wind resource is in your neighborhood. You can find a Wind Resource Map created by the U.S. Department of Energy here.

Another way to find out if there is enough wind in your area is to find Wind Speed Data from Airports. If the geography around your area is different from that which the airport is on, this might not be the most reliable source of information to use when making a decision however its a good first or second resource to use when initially deciding if wind power is worth pursuing.

Ultimately, the best way (and most expensive) is to buy a wind measurement device. You can monitor the wind power by finding a device online that can be placed on your roof this measures the strength of wind flows directly hitting your home.

Will The Government Allow it?

Before you get too excited with the thought of wind turbines spinning in your back yard, make sure youre not breaking any rules by setting them up. There are zoning restrictions in certain areas that may prevent you from building a wind power system. For example, it could be that people in your area dont want wind turbines obstructing their views. Another common issue that people have with wind turbines is the noise they make- if you live in a really dense area, these are plain unfeasible.

If you want to find out if zoning restrictions prohibit you from creating a small wind energy system, reach out to your local building inspector he or she is probably more than familiar with the zoning restrictions around different off-the-grid forms of energy.

Is it Financially Responsible?

Once youve figured out if its possible to set up a wind energy system, look into how much it will cost you, and the savings youll accrue over time by being off-the-grid.

Create a spreadsheet and list out the following line items:

  • Cost of Setting Up Wind Energy System
  • Savings
  • Potential Cash Flow Generated Through System
  • Energy Output from System
  • Electrical Bill Comparisons
  • ROI (When you will receive your investment back and begin earning money)


Once youve collected all this data, look at the cost of setting it up, how much youll save on an annual basis by not relying on government power, and how much youll begin to make once youve recouped the initial cost of setting everything up.

If its economically feasible- it might mean wind energy is the right off-the-grid solution for you.
